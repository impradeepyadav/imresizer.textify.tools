3. Advanced Features: DPI, Rotate and Filters

While basic resizing changes width and height, professional forms often mention DPI (Dots Per Inch). A standard screen image is 72 DPI, but printable forms require 200 to 300 DPI.

What does ImResizer do?

When you select "cm" or "mm" in our tool, we automatically calculate the required pixels based on the DPI setting (default 300). This ensures that when your application is printed by authorities, your photo remains sharp and clear, avoiding rejection. We maintain 300 DPI minimum for the highest quality output.

Filters & Rotation

If your scanned image is sideways, use the Left/Right Rotate buttons. If your signature looks grey, use the Signature Mode filter to make the background white and text dark.

4. Why is 20KB to 50KB Photo Size Standard?

Most government portals like SSC, UPSC, and State Boards handle millions of applications. To prevent server crashes and ensure fast loading, they enforce strict limits like 20KB to 50KB.

If your photo is 2MB, it takes 40x more storage and bandwidth. ImResizer uses intelligent compression to reduce file weight while keeping the face clear, ensuring your form accepts the file instantly without "File too large" errors. Our algorithm prioritizes image quality even when targeting small file sizes.

5. How to Take a Perfect Passport Photo at Home?

You don't need a studio. Follow these tips for a reject-proof photo:

  • Lighting: Stand facing a window. Natural daylight is best. Avoid flash to prevent shadows behind ears.
  • Background: Use a plain white wall or sheet. If you don't have one, use our Grayscale/Signature filter (for docs) or crop tightly.
  • Position: Keep the camera at eye level. Look straight. Both ears should be visible.
  • No Selfies: Ask someone else to click or use a timer. Selfies distort facial features (fish-eye effect).

6. Common Mistakes to Avoid (Photo Rejection)

Avoid these top 3 mistakes to ensure your application is safe:

  1. Inverted/Blurry Images: Always check if the text (Name/Date) is readable and not mirrored. Use our 'Flip' tool if needed.
  2. Wrong Aspect Ratio: Don't stretch a square photo into a rectangle. It makes the face look thin/fat. Use our Crop Tool first to set the frame, then resize.
  3. Wearing Accessories: Caps, sunglasses, and masks are strictly prohibited in official photos. Spectacles are allowed only if there is no flash reflection (glare).

7. Troubleshooting: Why Was My Photo Rejected?

Even after resizing, photos get rejected. Here are common reasons and fixes:

  • Blurred Face: You compressed the image too much (e.g., trying to fit a 5MB HD photo into 10KB). Fix: Crop the face closer before compressing.
  • Background Issue: SSC requires a clear, light background without glasses/caps. Fix: Use a proper source photo.
  • Wrong Extension: Form asked for JPG but you uploaded PNG. Fix: Our tool automatically exports as JPG universally compatible format (unless you select PNG/PDF).
  • Dimensions Mismatch: You uploaded 3.5x4.5cm where 4x5cm was needed. Fix: Check the official notification and use our Width/Height inputs.
